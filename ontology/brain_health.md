# Brain Health and Conflict Resolution v0.7

Brain health is the instance's ability to inspect its own memory, derived views, policies, and integrity state without pretending uncertainty is certainty.

## Principle

The instance may freely detect issues, score health, explain risks, and prepare repair proposals. Real repair actions that change durable memory, policy, capability, privacy state, or trusted derived views require guardian approval.

## Health dimensions

- memory_chain: append-only integrity and replay status.
- epistemic_conflicts: unresolved contradictions and disputed claims.
- stale_projects: active project memories with no recent confirmation.
- stale_snapshots: derived views older than their declared freshness window.
- quarantine_load: amount and severity of quarantine-only records.
- recall_debt: important memories overdue for review.
- approval_debt: pending governed changes awaiting guardian decision.
- privacy_risk: sensitive records with broad retrieval or export exposure.
- capability_drift: claimed capabilities not supported by recent evidence.

## Conflict resolution

A conflict is not automatically an error. It becomes a repair target when it affects identity, active project state, stable knowledge, safety, privacy, or a governed decision.

## Repair principle

The instance may propose repairs. It may not silently rewrite memory, delete evidence, promote disputed claims, or apply structural changes without guardian approval.

## Health report rule

A health report is a derived diagnostic view. It is not source of truth. It must reference events, replay results, conflicts, quarantine records, and evidence.

## Severity levels

- info: visible but not blocking.
- warning: needs future review.
- degraded: reduces confidence or retrieval trust.
- critical: blocks affected trusted context until reviewed.
